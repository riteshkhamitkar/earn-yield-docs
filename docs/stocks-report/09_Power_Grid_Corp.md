## Rank #9 | Energy & Grid Infrastructure | Score: 15.3/20

## 1. Executive Summary

**BUY | Fair Value: ~INR 350 | Current: INR 291 | Upside: 20% + 4.3% dividend yield**

Power Grid Corporation of India is India's central transmission utility and one of the largest power transmission companies in the world — owning and operating 180,000+ circuit kilometers of transmission lines and 282 substations. As a Navratna PSU, Power Grid operates as a **regulated monopoly** with guaranteed 15.5% ROE on regulated equity, providing unusual earnings visibility. The company offers a **4.3% dividend yield** — among the highest in quality Indian large-caps — backed by stable cash flows and a government-mandated dividend policy. Trading at just **14.5x trailing earnings** (vs. 20x+ for comparable global utilities) and **20% below its all-time high**, Power Grid offers defensive investors an attractive combination of income stability and modest capital appreciation. The energy transition — renewable integration, green hydrogen, EV charging, and smart grids — creates significant growth optionality beyond the core transmission business.

---

## 2. Business Overview

Power Grid operates India's interstate and inter-regional power transmission network:

**Core Business (~90% of revenue):**
- **Transmission services**: Owning and operating high-voltage transmission lines (765kV, 400kV, 220kV)
- **Substation operations**: 282 substations with 551,961 MVA transformation capacity
- **System operations**: National load despatch center and regional load despatch centers

**Growth Businesses (~10% of revenue):**
- **Telecom (PowerTel)**: 60,000+ km optical fiber network on transmission towers
- **Consultancy services**: Transmission project consulting globally
- **Smart grid/Metering**: AMI rollout, smart city projects
- **EV charging**: Setting up charging stations across highways
- **Data centers**: Leveraging land bank and power availability
- **Green hydrogen**: Pilot projects for hydrogen production

## 3. Industry Analysis

India's power sector is undergoing massive transformation:

- **Installed capacity**: 450+ GW; target 500 GW non-fossil by 2030
- **Renewable integration**: 400 GW solar/wind by 2030 requires massive transmission build-out
- **Green corridors**: Dedicated transmission lines for renewable energy zones
- **Grid modernization**: Smart grids, energy storage, demand response
- **EV infrastructure**: 2 crore EVs by 2030; charging infrastructure needed
- **Per capita consumption**: 1,300 kWh vs. 10,000+ kWh in developed nations — massive growth runway
- **Regulatory framework**: CERC guarantees 15.5% ROE on regulated equity

**Power Grid is the backbone of India's energy infrastructure** — every power plant, every renewable project, every EV charger connects to the grid.
